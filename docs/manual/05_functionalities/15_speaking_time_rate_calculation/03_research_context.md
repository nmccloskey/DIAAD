# Speaking-Time Rate Calculation Research Context

Speaking-time normalization helps compare discourse samples that differ in length. A raw count can be meaningful, but a per-minute rate answers a different question: how frequently a behavior or unit occurred during the measured speaking time.

## Why Rates Matter

Two participants may produce the same number of complete utterances, words, or target vocabulary items in very different amounts of time. Rate measures can make those differences visible.

Rates are especially useful when:

- sample durations vary substantially;
- the research question concerns efficiency or production density;
- raw totals need to be interpreted alongside opportunities for production;
- a project combines samples from tasks with different speaking-time demands.

## What Rates Do Not Solve

Rates depend on the quality of the speaking-time measurement. DIAAD does not measure speaking time from audio or transcripts. Users must enter time values.

Rates also do not make different constructs equivalent. Words per minute, complete utterances per minute, POWERS measures per minute, and target-vocabulary tokens per minute describe different dimensions of discourse. Interpret them with the module's coding rules and research context.

## Reporting

When reporting rate-based results, specify:

- how speaking time was measured;
- whether values were entered in seconds;
- which speakers or utterances were included;
- which numerator was rate-normalized;
- whether raw totals were also inspected.
