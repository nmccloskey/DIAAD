# Word Counting Research Context

Word counting sounds simple, but research word counts often depend on protocol-specific decisions. DIAAD therefore treats word counting as a human-reviewed workflow with automated first-pass support, not as a fully self-validating text metric.

## Human Review

Some discourse-analysis protocols exclude material such as repetitions, part-word repetitions, nonword fillers, prompt-only responses, or off-task commentary. Those decisions can be difficult to infer from text alone. DIAAD's first-pass counts can make coding more efficient, but users should review the output against the project's word-counting rules.

## Difference From Target Vocabulary Coverage

Word counting estimates total countable language. Target Vocabulary Coverage estimates production of a configured target lexicon. Both may involve tokens and counts, but their constructs are different.

For that reason, a sample can have a high word count and low target-vocabulary coverage, or strong target-vocabulary coverage in relatively few words. Rates normalize measures by speaking time, but they do not make the measures equivalent.

## Reliability

Word-count reliability evaluates agreement between primary and reliability counts. The module includes summary and report outputs, but interpretation depends on the project's tolerance for count differences and the protocol used by coders.
